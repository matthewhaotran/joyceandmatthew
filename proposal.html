Proposal Story

Joyce

I always thought I would see Matthew’s proposal a mile away. For previous surprises, I would be able to pick up on his nervous tics or random conversation (weirder than normal) that would raise flags but this time around, I’ll say it.. he (unfortunately) got me good. Lol

Matthew concocted the plan of a fancy dinner party that we would be attending in Paris. He described the dinner as an “interactive experience” wherein we would both help cook the meals and then eat what we prepared. We had reservations for a similar pasta-making class in Rome, so I figured it was the same idea. The evening of the dinner party we were to meet the hostess at a bridge before we were led to the dinner.

Walking up to the Debilly Footbridge, I noted on the beautiful view of the Eiffel Tower and how we should rush to snap some pictures before our dinner. The bridge was empty with the exception of a few bystanders and some peddlers selling locks and Eiffel Tower replicas. We got to the railing of the bridge to attempt a selfie capturing the tower in the back. A nearby professional photographer offered to take a picture for us and I thought “SCORE! A pro is taking free pictures for us on our phone!”

After handing us back our phone, Matthew shakily takes my hands and turns to face me. He says “Get ready, this is happening.” Immediately my thoughts are racing, “What?! What is going- Now?? WHAT!!??”

“Wait,” I say, “noo, wait wait wait. What?” And before I could gather my thoughts and muster a complete sentence Matthew gets down on one knee and presents me with a glistening ring. I’m all smiles, “yes!” I exclaim, oh wait Matthew hadn’t asked me anything yet. AH. I’m so flustered, a moment later he asks me the magical question to which I reply “of course, yes.”

The photographer came up to us and it turns out that Matthew hired her to take pictures for us. We spent the next hour taking pictures and making googly eyes at each other. We ended the night with a delicious meal at a nice restaurant next to the bridge where he proposed.

Bravo to the perfect proposal and night, fiancé! I loved every moment of it : )

Matthew

To start, the overall proposal was a complete success because it was both beautiful and a complete surprise. Planning was both easy and hard. It was easy because Joyce was quite oblivious to it all. She bought into the idea that’d probably propose in 2020. It was hard because I love sharing everything with her and it was hard to keep my mouth shut leading up to the proposal, especially during the trip because she is the only person I am talking to on a day to day basis. Admittedly, I told more than a handful of other people that I’d propose to her in Paris.

I got help from my mom, dad, Brittany, Ryan, Nancy, and Phung on picking out the ring. Ultimately, we all agreed on the right ring for Joyce. I set up the photographer weeks in advance through Google, went on some video calls with her to talk about us and our story, and planned the perfect ruse to allow our photographer to capture the moment perfectly.

We met on a bridge overlooking Tour Eiffel, where we walked and stopped to take some pictures with the landmark. My photographer, looking like an amateur tourist photographer, tapped me on the shoulder and asked if we wanted her to take a photo for us with our phone. We obliged and after the quick photo with my phone, she handed the phone back and stepped away. At that moment, I looked at Joyce and told her I wanted to ask something very important. Once she realized what was going on, I dropped to one knee, made sure I didn’t drop the ring through the cracks of the bridge and watched as her smile and eyes lit up.

Our photographer captured the candid moment perfectly (we didn’t need to take any posed proposal pictures!) and followed up with a full photoshoot and captured us perfectly as we were both elated from the proposal. I definitely will never forget any second of that moment.
