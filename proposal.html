Matthew

To start, the overall proposal was a complete success because it was both beautiful and a complete surprise. Planning was both easy and hard. It was easy because Joyce was quite oblivious to it all. She bought into the idea that’d probably propose in 2020. It was hard because I love sharing everything with her and it was hard to keep my mouth shut leading up to the proposal, especially during the trip because she is the only person I am talking to on a day to day basis. Admittedly, I told more than a handful of other people that I’d propose to her in Paris.

I got help from my mom, dad, Brittany, Ryan, Nancy, and Phung on picking out the ring. Ultimately, we all agreed on the right ring for Joyce. I set up the photographer weeks in advance through Google, went on some video calls with her to talk about us and our story, and planned the perfect ruse to allow our photographer to capture the moment perfectly.

We met on a bridge overlooking Tour Eiffel, where we walked and stopped to take some pictures with the landmark. My photographer, looking like an amateur tourist photographer, tapped me on the shoulder and asked if we wanted her to take a photo for us with our phone. We obliged and after the quick photo with my phone, she handed the phone back and stepped away. At that moment, I looked at Joyce and told her I wanted to ask something very important. Once she realized what was going on, I dropped to one knee, made sure I didn’t drop the ring through the cracks of the bridge and watched as her smile and eyes lit up.

Our photographer captured the candid moment perfectly (we didn’t need to take any posed proposal pictures!) and followed up with a full photoshoot and captured us perfectly as we were both elated from the proposal. I definitely will never forget any second of that moment.
